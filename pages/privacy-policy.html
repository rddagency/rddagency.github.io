Section 01

Who We Are

Razi Digital Agency Ltd. (trading as RDD Agency) is a company registered in England and Wales (Company No. 10XXXXXXX), with its registered office at Level 22, One Canada Square, Canary Wharf, London E14 5AB, United Kingdom.

RDD Agency is a global cybersecurity firm providing penetration testing, red team operations, managed detection and response (MDR), threat intelligence, digital forensics, incident response, and cloud security services to enterprise and government clients in 47 countries.

For the purposes of UK GDPR and EU GDPR, RDD Agency is the Data Controller of personal data collected through our website, client portals, and business relationships. Where we process data on behalf of clients, we act as a Data Processor under a formal Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

Data Protection Officer (DPO): RDD Agency has appointed a Data Protection Officer in accordance with Article 37 of UK/EU GDPR. You may contact our DPO directly at dpo@rddagency.com for any privacy-related concerns.

We collect personal data through the following methods:

Section 05

Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR and EU GDPR, we are required to have a valid legal basis for processing your personal data. We rely on the following lawful bases:

Legal BasisWhen We Rely on It
Contractual Necessity
Article 6(1)(b)		 Processing your data is necessary to enter into or perform a contract with you — for example, delivering a penetration test or incident response engagement.
Legitimate Interests
Article 6(1)(f)		 We have a legitimate business interest in processing data for website analytics, business development, fraud prevention, improving our services, and cybersecurity operations — where these interests are not overridden by your rights.
Legal Obligation
Article 6(1)(c)		 Where processing is required to comply with applicable law — such as anti-money laundering obligations, sanctions screening, or responding to lawful requests by regulatory or law enforcement authorities.
Consent
Article 6(1)(a)		 Where you have expressly opted in — such as subscribing to our threat intelligence newsletter, agreeing to marketing communications, or accepting non-essential cookies.
Vital Interests
Article 6(1)(d)		 In rare circumstances where processing is necessary to protect life — for example, during a critical incident response involving imminent threat to human safety.

Where we rely on Legitimate Interests, we conduct and document a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) to ensure your rights and interests are appropriately balanced. You may request a copy of any relevant LIA by contacting our DPO.

Section 07

Data Sharing & Transfers

RDD Agency does not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties. We share data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers and sub-processors — Trusted third-party vendors who assist us in operating our business (e.g., cloud infrastructure providers, CRM systems, secure document management, email platforms). All sub-processors are bound by Data Processing Agreements meeting UK/EU GDPR standards.
  • Professional advisors — Lawyers, accountants, auditors, and insurers who require access to data to provide professional services, subject to confidentiality obligations.
  • Regulatory and law enforcement authorities — Where required by law, court order, or legitimate regulatory request. We will notify you where legally permitted to do so.
  • Business transactions — In the event of a merger, acquisition, restructuring, or sale of assets, personal data may be transferred to the relevant parties, subject to equivalent privacy protections.
  • With your consent — For any other purpose, only with your explicit prior consent.

We never share client data with intelligence agencies, advertising networks, or data brokers. Our business model is built on client trust. Unauthorised disclosure of client data would be a fundamental breach of our professional obligations.

Section 08

International Transfers

RDD Agency operates globally with offices in the UK, USA, UAE, Singapore, Germany, and Brazil. Personal data may be transferred to, stored in, or processed in countries outside your country of residence, including countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA) and the UK.

Where we transfer personal data outside the UK or EEA, we ensure an equivalent level of protection is afforded by relying on one or more of the following safeguards:

  • Transfers to countries recognised by the UK ICO or European Commission as providing an adequate level of data protection (e.g., adequacy decisions).
  • Use of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the UK ICO (International Data Transfer Agreements, IDTAs) or the European Commission.
  • Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) where applicable for intra-group transfers.
  • Implementation of supplementary technical and organisational measures (TOMs) including encryption, pseudonymisation, and access controls.

You may request a copy of the safeguards we apply to your data by contacting privacy@rddagency.com.

Section 10

Data Security

As a cybersecurity firm, data security is not merely a legal obligation — it is the foundation of our business. We apply enterprise-grade technical and organisational measures (TOMs) to protect personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

  • Encryption at rest and in transit — AES-256 encryption at rest; TLS 1.3 enforced for all data in transit.
  • Zero-trust architecture — Strict identity verification, least-privilege access controls, and continuous device posture assessment across all internal systems.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) — Mandatory across all internal systems, client portals, and third-party platforms handling personal data.
  • 24/7 internal SOC monitoring — Our own Security Operations Centre monitors our infrastructure in real time, providing us the same protection we deliver to clients.
  • Annual penetration testing — Our own systems undergo independent red team assessments by CREST-certified third parties annually.
  • ISO 27001 certification — Our information security management system (ISMS) is certified to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 by an accredited third-party auditor.
  • Staff training — All personnel complete mandatory annual data protection and security awareness training.
  • Data breach response — We maintain a tested Incident Response plan with defined obligations to notify the ICO within 72 hours of a qualifying breach, and affected individuals without undue delay.

Data Breach Notification: In the event of a personal data breach affecting your rights and freedoms, RDD Agency will notify you without undue delay as required under UK GDPR Article 34. To report a suspected breach, contact ir@rddagency.com immediately.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, EU GDPR, CCPA, and other applicable laws, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data. We will respond to all valid requests within 30 days (extendable to 60 days for complex requests, with notice).

👁
Right of Access
Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you (Subject Access Request / SAR). Free of charge in most cases.
✏️
Right to Rectification
Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
🗑
Right to Erasure
Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten") where there is no overriding legal reason to retain it.
🔒
Right to Restriction
Request we limit how we process your data while a dispute or complaint is being resolved.
📦
Right to Portability
Receive a copy of your data in a structured, machine-readable format (where processing is consent- or contract-based).
🚫
Right to Object
Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing purposes. We will cease unless compelling grounds exist.
🤖
Automated Decision-Making
Request human review of any purely automated decisions that significantly affect you. We do not use fully automated decision-making for consequential matters.
↩️
Right to Withdraw Consent
Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time. This does not affect the lawfulness of prior processing.
⚖️
Right to Lodge a Complaint
Complain to the UK ICO (ico.org.uk) or your national supervisory authority if you believe we have mishandled your data.

To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to privacy@rddagency.com. We may need to verify your identity before processing the request. We will not charge a fee unless requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive.

California residents (CCPA): You additionally have the right to know the categories of personal information collected, the right to opt-out of any sale (note: we do not sell personal information), and the right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights. Submit requests via the same email address above.

Cookies & Tracking

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies. Cookies are small text files placed on your device to help us provide a better user experience and understand how our site is used.

Cookie TypePurposeConsent Required
Strictly NecessaryEssential for the website to function — session management, security tokens, load balancing. Cannot be disabled.No — exempt under PECR
Performance / AnalyticsAnonymised data on how visitors use our site (page views, session duration, bounce rate). We use privacy-first analytics with IP anonymisation.Yes — consent required

We do not use third-party advertising cookies for consumer profiling. Any analytics data is aggregated and never sold to third parties.

Section 13

Children's Privacy

RDD Agency's services and website are directed exclusively at business professionals and enterprises. We do not knowingly collect, use, or process personal data from individuals under the age of 18.

If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from a person under 18, we will take immediate steps to delete that data from our records. If you believe we have collected data from a minor, please contact us immediately at privacy@rddagency.com.
