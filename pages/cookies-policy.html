Section 01

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device — computer, tablet, or mobile phone — when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide analytical information to site owners.

Cookies are not programs. They cannot execute code, carry viruses, or access information on your device beyond what was stored in them. They can only be read by the website or service that set them, subject to the same-origin policy enforced by your browser.

Beyond traditional cookies, we may also use related technologies including:

  • Session storage — Temporary data stored in your browser memory that is deleted when you close the tab or browser window.
  • Local storage — Similar to cookies but stored locally in your browser with no automatic expiry or server transmission.
  • Web beacons / pixel tags — Tiny invisible images embedded in pages or emails that confirm when content has been loaded or an email has been opened.
  • Server logs — Automatically recorded data including IP address, browser type, pages visited, and timestamps, stored on our web servers.
  • Device fingerprinting — We do not use device fingerprinting or any technique designed to track you without your consent across sessions.

Throughout this policy, we use the word "cookies" to refer collectively to all of the above technologies unless stated otherwise.

Section 02

Why We Use Cookies

RDD Agency uses cookies on this website for the following core purposes:

  • Security — To protect the integrity of user sessions, prevent cross-site request forgery (CSRF), and detect and block abusive or bot-driven traffic.
  • Functionality — To remember your preferences, display the site correctly, and enable features such as contact forms and the client portal login.
  • Analytics — To understand which pages are most visited, how users navigate the site, and where we can improve the experience. All analytics data is anonymised before processing.
  • Marketing — With your consent, to display relevant RDD Agency content to business professionals who have previously visited our site via professional networks such as LinkedIn.
  • Performance — To measure site load times, identify slow-loading pages, and optimise the technical performance of the site.

Our Commitment: We do not use cookies to build personal profiles for sale to third parties. We do not use cookies to track you across unrelated websites. We do not place any non-essential cookies until you have actively consented to them through our Cookie Consent Manager.

Section 04

Strictly Necessary Cookies

Strictly necessary cookies are essential for the website to function correctly. They enable core features such as page navigation, form submissions, secure sessions, and access to protected areas of the site. Under UK PECR and EU ePrivacy Directive, these cookies are exempt from the consent requirement.

These cookies do not collect any information about you that could be used for marketing or to track your activity on other websites. They are first-party cookies only.

  • Session management and user authentication (client portal)
  • CSRF protection tokens to prevent cross-site request forgery attacks
  • Load balancing to distribute traffic across our server infrastructure
  • Cookie consent preferences storage — to remember that you have seen and responded to our consent banner
  • Security tokens required to submit contact and report forms
  • TLS session resumption tokens to optimise encrypted connection performance

Why these cannot be disabled: Disabling strictly necessary cookies would prevent core site functions from working, including the ability to submit enquiries, log in to the client portal, or use our report submission forms. The site would not load correctly without them.

Section 05

Performance Cookies

Performance cookies collect anonymised information about how visitors use our website — which pages they visit, how long they stay, what links they click, and how they found the site. This data helps us understand user behaviour and improve the site experience.

How we protect your privacy in analytics:

  • IP anonymisation — The final octet of all IP addresses is removed before any storage or processing.
  • No cross-site tracking — Our analytics do not track you across other websites.
  • Data aggregation — We review aggregated statistics, not individual user journeys.
  • No advertising use — Analytics data is never shared with advertising networks or used to serve you targeted ads.
  • Data minimisation — We only collect the minimum data needed to understand site usage patterns.
  • Retention limit — Aggregated analytics data is retained for a maximum of 13 months, in line with ICO guidance.

We use privacy-first analytics configured with full IP anonymisation and data residency in the UK/EU. We do not use Google Analytics Universal Analytics (which sends data to US servers). Our analytics platform stores all data within the European Economic Area.

Section 06

Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow the website to remember choices you have made and provide enhanced, more personalised features. These cookies are not strictly necessary for the site to work but significantly improve your experience.

Examples of what functional cookies enable on the RDD Agency website:

  • Language and region preferences — Remembering your preferred language or geographic region so you see the most relevant content on return visits.
  • Form pre-population — With your consent, remembering your name and email address so you do not need to re-enter them on subsequent form submissions.
  • Consent manager state — Storing which cookie categories you have accepted or rejected so we do not show you the consent banner repeatedly.
  • Chat widget preferences — If you have interacted with our live chat support, remembering your conversation thread and preferences.
  • Navigation state — Remembering which sections of a long page you have expanded or collapsed during a session.

Functional cookies do not track your browsing activity across other websites and do not contain personally identifiable information beyond what you have chosen to provide.

Section 07

Marketing Cookies

With your explicit consent, we use a limited set of marketing cookies to support B2B retargeting — that is, displaying relevant RDD Agency content and advertisements to business professionals who have previously visited our site on platforms such as LinkedIn.

We are deliberately narrow in our use of marketing cookies:

  • We use LinkedIn Insight Tag only, for B2B audience retargeting on LinkedIn. We do not use Facebook Pixel, Google Ads, or any consumer advertising network.
  • Marketing cookies are only set after you have actively opted in. They are never placed on first visit without consent.
  • We do not build individual consumer profiles or sell data to data brokers.
  • LinkedIn Insight Tag data is governed by LinkedIn's Privacy Policy. You can opt out of LinkedIn personalised advertising at any time in your LinkedIn account settings.

No consumer advertising: RDD Agency does not run consumer advertising campaigns. Our marketing cookies are used exclusively for reaching cybersecurity decision-makers and enterprise IT professionals on professional platforms. We do not track consumer browsing behaviour, health data, location, or any sensitive category for advertising purposes.

Section 11

Browser Controls

In addition to our Consent Manager, all modern browsers allow you to manage cookies directly through your browser settings. You can choose to block all cookies, block third-party cookies only, or delete cookies that have already been set. Here is how to access cookie settings in the most common browsers:

🌐
Google Chrome
Open Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data. From here you can block third-party cookies, clear all cookies, or manage exceptions per site.
🦊
Mozilla Firefox
Open Settings → Privacy & Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection. Choose Strict mode to block all third-party tracking cookies. Use Manage Exceptions for per-site control.
🧭
Apple Safari
Open Preferences → Privacy → Enable Prevent cross-site tracking and Block all cookies. On iOS: Settings → Safari → Privacy & Security.
🔷
Microsoft Edge
Open Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data. Use Tracking prevention set to Balanced or Strict to block most third-party trackers.
🟠
Opera
Open Settings → Advanced → Privacy & security → Site Settings → Cookies and site data. Supports per-site cookie blocking.
🛡
Brave Browser
Brave blocks third-party cookies and trackers by default via Shields. To adjust: click the Brave icon in the address bar → adjust Cross-site cookies blocked per site.

Please note: Blocking all cookies via your browser — including strictly necessary cookies — will prevent core site functionality including form submissions, portal login, and security features from working correctly. We recommend using our Consent Manager (Section 10) for granular control without disrupting essential functionality.

Additional opt-out tools for specific tracking networks:

  • LinkedIn Ad Preferences — linkedin.com/psettings/advertising — opt out of interest-based advertising on LinkedIn.
  • Your Online Choices (EU) — youronlinechoices.eu — opt out of participating advertising networks in one place.
  • Network Advertising Initiative (US) — optout.networkadvertising.org — manage advertising opt-outs across member networks.
  • All About Cookies — allaboutcookies.org — independent resource on managing cookies across all browsers and devices.
Section 12

Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that sends a signal to websites requesting that your browsing activity not be tracked. There is currently no agreed technical standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, and the W3C's DNT specification was abandoned in 2019.

At RDD Agency, we respect the intent behind DNT. When we detect a DNT signal in your browser, we:

  • Do not activate any performance analytics cookies by default.
  • Do not activate any marketing or retargeting cookies.
  • Treat your visit as if you had selected "Reject Non-Essential" in our Consent Manager.

Strictly necessary cookies are still placed regardless of DNT signals, as these are essential for the site to function and do not track you across sessions or sites.

If you use a DNT-enabled browser and would like to enable performance or functional cookies for an improved experience, you can explicitly opt in via our Consent Manager in Section 10. Your explicit consent overrides the DNT default.

Section 14

Data Protection

Cookie-related data processing at RDD Agency is conducted in accordance with our full Privacy Policy, UK GDPR, EU GDPR, and the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003 (PECR).

Key data protection principles applied to our cookie use:

  • Lawfulness — Strictly necessary cookies rely on the legitimate interest basis under PECR. All other cookies require your explicit, freely given, specific consent before being placed.
  • Transparency — This Cookie Policy provides full, clear disclosure of every cookie we use, its purpose, duration, and the data it processes.
  • Data minimisation — Cookies are designed to collect the minimum data necessary for their stated purpose. We regularly audit cookies to remove any that are no longer required.
  • Purpose limitation — Cookie data is used only for the purposes stated in this policy. Analytics data is never repurposed for marketing without fresh consent.
  • Storage limitation — Cookie lifespans are set to the minimum necessary. We do not use persistent identifiers beyond what is needed.
  • Security — All cookies set by RDD Agency use the Secure, HttpOnly, and SameSite=Strict flags where applicable to prevent interception or cross-site misuse.

For full details on how we handle personal data, your rights under UK/EU GDPR, and how to submit a Subject Access Request, please refer to our Privacy Policy.

Section 15

Policy Updates

We review this Cookie Policy at minimum every 6 months and after every scheduled cookie audit. The policy is also updated whenever:

  • We add, change, or remove any cookies or tracking technologies from our site.
  • A third-party service we use changes its cookie behaviour or privacy policy.
  • Applicable law, regulatory guidance (ICO, EDPB), or industry standards change.
  • We launch new features on the site that require new cookies.

For material changes — such as the addition of a new cookie category or a new third-party tracking service — we will reset cookie consents and present you with the updated consent banner on your next visit. This ensures your consent is always informed and current.

Non-material changes (for example, corrections, clarifications, or minor rewording) will not trigger a consent reset. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this page will reflect all revisions, material or otherwise.
