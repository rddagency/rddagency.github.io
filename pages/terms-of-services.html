Section 06

Scope & Authorisation

This section is critical. All offensive security activities — including penetration testing, red team operations, vulnerability assessments, and security audits — are performed exclusively within the scope and authorisation defined in the signed Statement of Work and accompanying Rules of Engagement (ROE) document.

Legal Notice: Unauthorised access to computer systems is a criminal offence under the Computer Misuse Act 1990 (UK), the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (USA), and equivalent legislation globally. RDD Agency only performs offensive security testing with explicit written authorisation from the legal owner or duly authorised representative of the target systems. Any request to test systems without such authorisation will be refused and may be reported to relevant authorities.

The Client warrants and represents that they:

  • Are the legal owner of, or have explicit written authorisation from the legal owner of, all systems, networks, applications, and data included within the agreed scope.
  • Have the authority to grant RDD Agency permission to conduct the agreed testing activities.
  • Will provide written confirmation of authorisation ("Get Out of Jail" letter) before any testing commences, on request.
  • Will promptly notify RDD Agency of any systems or assets that must be excluded from testing scope.
  • Understand that testing activities may cause unintended service disruption if conducted in production environments and accept responsibility for this risk where they have approved such testing.

RDD Agency will immediately suspend testing and notify the Client if activities outside the agreed scope are discovered or if testing poses unacceptable risk to systems or data. The Client will not hold RDD Agency liable for issues arising from the Client's failure to accurately define or communicate the scope.
